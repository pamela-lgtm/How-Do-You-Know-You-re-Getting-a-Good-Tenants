How Do You Know You're Getting a Good Tenant?

It's the question I hear most from first-time owners. After more than 20 years, here's exactly what we look for — and how it protects your investment from the very first day.

By Pamela Syvertson, Owner & Broker · Verandah Properties LLC

The question I hear most from first-time owners is some version of this: "How do I know I'm getting a good tenant?" It's the right question to ask — because the answer is what protects everything you've invested. Here's what two decades in this business have taught me actually matters.

1
The number-one indicator

Credit tells the truth

Credit is the single most reliable indicator we have. That might sound overly simple, but in my experience it holds true more consistently than almost anything else. How someone manages their credit — their payment history, their obligations, how they've handled financial commitments over time — tells you a great deal about how they conduct themselves across the board. It isn't just a score; it's a window into someone's character and accountability. Black swan events happen — 2008 reminded all of us of that — but day in and day out, credit history is the most dependable signal there is.

And we put real weight behind it: we report every on-time and late payment directly to Experian. Our residents know that — and that accountability works in your favor every single month. It also rewards good tenants by helping them build their credit, which is one more reason the right people want to rent from a Verandah-managed home.
2

Income tells the supporting story

We look for tenants whose gross monthly income is at least 2.5 to 3 times the monthly rent. Strong credit paired with verified income is the combination that genuinely protects your investment — one confirms character, the other confirms capacity.

3

Rental history adds context

A solid track record with previous landlords — how they maintained the property, whether they communicated well, how they left it — rounds out the picture that credit and income begin. Patterns repeat, and a tenant's past tells us a great deal about the tenancy ahead.

4

The details most people overlook

How someone communicates during the application process, how quickly they respond, whether they ask thoughtful questions — these soft signals have saved owners from difficult situations more times than I can count. Twenty years teaches you to pay attention to them.

The standard every applicant is held to

Every applicant is measured against our stringent resident selection criteria, and only those who meet our standards are approved — no exceptions. This is how we protect you, your property, and the integrity of the tenancy from day one.
